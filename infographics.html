Infographics

A collection of Infographics made with Adobe Illustrator and HTML/CSS

Dynamic Timeline

Crafted with HTML and CSS, leveraging flexbox, I designed a timeline infographic that seamlessly adapts to multiple screen sizes, ensuring a responsive and visually compelling user experience. Prioritizing accessibility, the timeline was tailored for screen readers, enhancing inclusivity by providing a comprehensive and easily navigable narrative for all users. This infographic was adapted for Canvas using an iframe to integrate the graphic within the course content.

HTML CSS Flexbox Adobe Color Wheel

High vs Low Fidelity Prototype

Created for an Indiana University Digital Design course. I utilized Adobe Illustrator to craft an informative infographic comparing High and Low Fidelity Prototypes. The visually engaging graphic served as a comprehensive reference for students, illustrating the key distinctions in the prototyping process, while using a unique color scheme to enhance the visual appeal.

Adobe Illustrator Adobe Color Wheel

Public Health Infastructure

Created for an Indiana University Public Health Course. I utilized Adobe Illustrator in order to create the vector layout and incorporated visually cohesive icons from Adobe Stock. To reinforce the connection with Indiana University, I integrated a color scheme that fit well with the content while maintaining a clear design within the context of public health information.
